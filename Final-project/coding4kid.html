Course Overview

Fundamentals of Coding for Kids" is designed as an introductory course for children aged 8-12. It focuses on the basics of computer programming through an interactive, playful, and visually engaging approach. The course uses platforms known for their child-friendly interfaces, such as Scratch or Blockly, which do not require previous coding knowledge and provide instant visual feedback that keeps young learners motivated.

Course Structure

  • Duration: 10 weeks (1 session per week, 1.5 hours per session)
  • Blended learning (combination of live online classes and self-paced projects)
  • Class Size: Small groups (up to 10 students per class) to ensure personalized attention

Learning Objectives

    By the end of this course, students will be able to:
  1. Understand Basic Programming Concepts: Learn fundamental concepts such as sequences, loops, conditionals, and variables.
  2. Develop Problem-Solving Skills: Enhance logical thinking by tackling progressively challenging tasks and debugging their own code.
  3. Create Projects: Build simple games and animations that encourage creativity and consolidate coding skills.
  4. Work Collaboratively: Participate in group projects that foster teamwork, sharing ideas, and communication skills.

Curriculum Highlights

  • Week 1-2: Introduction to the platform and basic coding blocks.
  • Week 3-4: Creating simple animations using basic coding constructs.
  • Week 5-6: Introduction to interactive programming and user inputs.
  • Week 7-8: Designing and programming basic games.
  • Week 9: Collaborative project work.
  • Week 10: Review and showcase of student projects.


Why Choose This Course?

Fundamentals of Coding for Kids" provides a safe, supportive, and stimulating environment for young learners to take their first steps into the world of programming. The course is carefully designed to balance educational outcomes with fun, ensuring that children not only learn valuable skills but also develop a passion for technology that can last a lifetime.
