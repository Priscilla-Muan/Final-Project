Course Overview

Robotics for Beginners" is an introductory course that immerses students in the fundamental principles of robotics. Through hands-on projects, participants will explore the interplay of programming, electronics, and mechanical systems that make up the building blocks of robots. This course is designed to demystify robotics technology and open the door to more advanced studies and applications in robotics.

Course Structure

  1. Introduction to Robotics
    • Understanding the role of robots in society
    • Overview of the components that make up a robot
  2. Basic Electronics
    • Learning about electricity and circuits
    • Hands-on: Building simple circuits on a breadboard
  3. Programming for Robotics
    • Introduction to programming using Arduino
    • Writing simple programs to control lights and sensors
  4. Mechanical Systems
    • Basics of mechanical engineering for robotics
    • Assembling gears and motors to create movement
  5. Sensor Integration
    • Exploring different types of sensors (touch, light, distance)
    • Incorporating sensors to allow robots to interact with their environment
  6. Robot Design Project
    • Final project where students design and build their own robot to solve a real-life problem
    • Presentations of projects to the class


Course Structure

  • Duration: 10 weeks (1 session per week, 1.5 hours per session)
  • Blended learning (combination of live online classes and self-paced projects)
  • Class Size: Small groups (up to 10 students per class) to ensure personalized attention


Learning Outcomes


By the end of this course, students will:

  • Have a solid foundation in the basic principles of robotics.
  • Be able to assemble and program a robot using standard components.
  • Understand how mechanical, electronic, and programming components integrate to create automated systems.
  • Develop critical thinking and problem-solving skills related to robotics.

Course Details:

  • Week 1-2: Introduction to Robotics and Basic Components
  • Week 3-4: Basic Circuitry and Microcontrollers
  • Week 5-6: Programming for Robotics
  • Week 7-8: Building a Mini Robot
  • Week 9: Collaborative Project Work
  • Week 10: Review and Showcase of Student Projects


Why Choose This Course?

  1. Build a Strong Foundation: Learn essential robotics skills in programming, electronics, and mechanics.
  2. Hands-On Learning: Engage in practical, project-based activities that reinforce theoretical knowledge.
  3. Develop Critical Skills: Enhance problem-solving, teamwork, and innovative thinking abilities.
  4. Real-World Applications: Understand the practical impact of robotics in various industries.
  5. Creativity and Collaboration: Work on collaborative projects that foster creativity and teamwork.
